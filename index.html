About the Workshop

Despite rapid progress in robot learning and multimodal foundation models, enabling robots to build robust spatial understanding of their environments remains a fundamental challenge for long-horizon decision-making and reliable interaction in complex real-world settings. Tasks such as manipulation in cluttered environments, multi-step task execution, and navigation in dynamic spaces require robots to understand the spatial structure of scenes, reason about object relationships, and anticipate how the environment may change in response to their actions.

Recent research has begun to address these challenges through advances in spatial perception, world models, multimodal representations, and vision–language–action learning. However, these efforts are often fragmented across different communities within robotics, machine learning, and embodied AI. As a result, there is a growing need to bring together researchers working on spatial understanding, predictive modeling, and embodied robot learning.

This workshop aims to provide a forum for discussing emerging approaches to spatial representations and world models for embodied robots. By bringing together experts in robot manipulation, navigation, multimodal learning, and large-scale robot learning systems, the workshop will explore how improved spatial understanding can enable more robust, generalizable, and scalable robotic systems operating in complex real-world environments.

Key topics include:

  • World models for robotic manipulation and navigation
  • Spatial intelligence and 3D scene understanding for embodied AI
  • Multimodal representations for robot learning
  • Vision–language–action models for physical AI
  • Predictive modeling and anticipation in dynamic environments
  • Long-horizon task planning with spatial reasoning
  • Foundation models for embodied robot learning
  • Sim-to-real transfer and scalable robot learning systems
  • Benchmarks and evaluation for spatial understanding in robotics

Call for Papers

We invite submissions of short papers (4 pages + references) on topics related to world models and spatial intelligence for physical AI. We welcome both novel research contributions and position/survey papers.

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • World models for robotic manipulation and navigation
  • Spatial intelligence and 3D scene understanding
  • Multimodal and vision–language–action representations
  • Predictive modeling for dynamic environments
  • Long-horizon planning with spatial reasoning
  • Foundation models for embodied agents
  • Sim-to-real transfer and scalable robot learning
  • Benchmarks and datasets for spatial understanding
  • Real-world deployment of physical AI systems

Submissions should follow the IEEE conference format and be submitted via OpenReview (link TBD).

Accepted papers will be presented as posters with spotlight talks. At least one author of each accepted paper must attend the workshop.

📄 Ken Goldberg — Bio

Ken Goldberg is President of the Robot Learning Foundation (organizer of the annual Conference on Robot Learning) and William S. Floyd Distinguished Chair of Engineering at UC Berkeley and Chief Scientist of Ambi Robotics and Jacobi Robotics. Ken leads research in robotics and automation: grasping, manipulation, and learning for applications in warehouses, industry, homes, agriculture, and robot-assisted surgery.

He is Professor of IEOR with appointments in EECS and Art Practice. Ken is Chair of the Berkeley AI Research (BAIR) Steering Committee (60 faculty) and is co-founder and Editor-in-Chief emeritus of the IEEE Transactions on Automation Science and Engineering (T-ASE). He has published ten US patents, over 400 refereed papers, and presented over 600 invited lectures to academic and corporate audiences.

📄 Tetsuya Ogata — Bio

Tetsuya Ogata received the B.S., M.S., and D.E. degrees in mechanical engineering from Waseda University, Tokyo, Japan. He was a Research Associate with Waseda University, a Research Scientist with the RIKEN Brain Science Institute, and an Associate Professor at Kyoto University. Since 2012, he has been a Professor with the Faculty of Science and Engineering at Waseda University.

He is a Joint-appointed Fellow with the National Institute of Advanced Industrial Science and Technology and a visiting professor at the National Institute of Informatics (NII). He has been the director of the Institute of AI and Robotics at Waseda University since 2020, the chairperson of the AI Robot Association (AIRoA), and the Research supervisor of JST CREST "Fundamentals and Core Technologies for Embodied AI" since 2025. His research interests include cognitive developmental robotics, VLA (Vision Language Action) models, and human–robot interaction. IEEE Fellow since 2026.
